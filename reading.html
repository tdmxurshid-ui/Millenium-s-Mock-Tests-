Urban Green Spaces

In recent years, city planners have increased their focus on urban green spaces such as parks, community gardens, and tree-lined streets. These areas improve air quality, reduce noise pollution, and provide essential recreational space for residents. Green spaces also contribute to better mental health by offering a peaceful escape from the busyness of city life.

Studies suggest that people living near parks are more likely to exercise regularly and feel socially connected. As a result, many local governments are investing in new green initiatives, including pocket parks, rooftop gardens, and green corridors that link neighborhoods.
